The Boy Scout motto is "Be Prepared". Established in 1907, this motto means that a Scout is always in a state of readiness—both in mind and body—to perform their duty, face challenges, and help others. It signifies being prepared for any emergency, accident, or situation that might occur. 

Meaning Behind "Be Prepared"
Mind: Disciplining yourself to be obedient and thinking through potential situations beforehand so you know the right thing to do, as described by Wikipedia.
Body: Training yourself to be strong, active, and capable of taking action when necessary. 

Related Scouting Principles
Slogan: "Do a Good Turn Daily".
Oath: "On my honor I will do my best To do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; To help other people at all times; To keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight."

The motto is often interpreted as being ready to assist in both everyday life and emergency situations.
